Show Crop Marks   
Crop Marks are important for cutting operators on full bleed rectangular artworks. "Proofs" may not need crop marks shown since Bleed/Safe lines are shown instead. Special shapes, such as envelopes, do not use Crop Marks. Envelopes can use Shape Objects to define spotter marks instead.
No      Yes    
Show Outside Border   
Similar to Crop Marks, when this setting is enabled, a hairline rectangle will be drawn on the outside perimeter of the printable area. This is located outside of the bleed area. This can be a useful indicator on slitting equipment to alert operators when the machines are not calibrated correctly. On slitting machines, scrap paper with crop marks may be discarded and artworks with white backgrounds may not give many clues when registration has gone bad. Generally, whenever Crop Marks are enabled, this setting should be as well.
No      Yes    
Grid Spacing  
This will set the distance between each row and column. If there are 4 rows and 3 columns then there would be 2 horizontal spaces and 3 vertical spaces. Distance is measured between the "Cut Lines" of each Artwork Unit. Therefore, adding extra "Bleed" will diminish the perceived spacing. Values are in Picas, decimals are OK. (72 Picas = 1 inch).

Bleed  
This will allow artwork to be printed beyond the Cut Line. Extra Quantity Control  
For certain products, it may be necessary to print a larger quantity than is ordered. A good reason for this is when you expect some of the product to go defective during a finishing process (such as converting printed sheets into envelopes). This setting is only effective for digital printing (not Gang Runs).

You can specify a percentage of extra to print, and then set a Max Limit on the total extras. For example, you may determine that 3 percent of envelopes get destroyed during the coverting process, but only when the first 1,000 are sent though. After the machine is tuned perfectly for the paper stock, etc, there may be little defects from that point forward. So you may set a Maximum Limit of 50 extra envelopes. Set Max Limit to zero for no limit.

You may also set the Extra Percentage to a negative value. That means less get printed than what was ordered. On very large orders it is unlikey customers will count every piece, and most printers specify final quantities to have a + / - error. This can be usefull if you change your card stock to be thicker, and the full quantity does not fit within a pre-made 5,000 piece box, etc. The Max Limit is always a postive value, but you can use it stop shorting at a certain amount. For example, if the Max Limit is set to 50, the system will never short the customer more than 50, regardless of how large the negative percentage is.

The Minimum Quantity means that no Extras will be printed until the Order Quantity reaches that level. The same goes for negative percentages, quantity will not be decreased until the order quantity exceeds the minimum. Set Minimum Quantity to zero for no minimum.

CMYK Blocks  
It is useful for printing press operators to have CMYK blocks printed in the scrap areas of the paper. This will help the figure out if a particular ink us running low.

CMYK Groups contain 1 or more CMYK blocks. You may control the distance between each block (Spacing), the block width/height. You can create as many groups as you want and put them on different parts of the sheet. For example, you may want a group printed across the top of the sheet, and another printed at the bottom of the sheet.

Coordinates, Block Width, Block Height, and Spacing are all values measured in inches.

Shapes  
You can draw many shapes on top of the PDF sheet with different colors and sizes for a variety of reasons. You may want to show customers where holes will be punched out on their artwork. Shapes can be drawn in scrap areas to assist in production, such as "Spotter Marks". Draw many shapes in a series to create a larger, more complex shape. You can put a Option Limiter on the shape so that it only gets rendered when a match is found on the Option & Choice Name Aliases (as displayed on the Open Order List Screen). For example an Envelope with "Windows - Yes" may want to show a rectangle where the hole will be punched out.

The Local Shape Container holds shapes that will be repeated in the same location for every artwork unit. Like a Circle in the same position of every business card. The Global Shape Container hold shapes that are drawn 1 time on the entire parent sheet. Like a rectangle on the top-left portion of the sheet (which contains 20 business cards).

0% "Alpha" means invisible, 100% means opaque. There are currently some limitations with generating opacity on PDF documents as well as RIPing the documents in production. For that reason, opacity will be rendered on PDF's at 100%. However, the Editing Tool may display "Local Shapes" on top of the artwork (with opacity settings), depending on the Option Limiters. Shapes can assist customers during the design process. The system will automatically check the "Proof" profile (a special profile name) at the time that artwork is being downloaded and will extract any "Local Shapes" off of the profile.

The registration points for rectangles lie on the lower-left corner. For circles, registration is in the center.

If multiple shapes overlap, you can control the stacking sequence by putting a prefix in the "Remarks" field. For example a Remark starting with "A" would be drawn on top of a remark started with "B".
