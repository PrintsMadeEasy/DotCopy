Parent Product  
Products can be closely related and in some circumstances the system would prefer not to list every single derivative ... such as ... 4"x 6" Postcards, 5.5"x 8.5" Postcards, 4"x 6" Variable Data Postcards, and 5.5" x 8.5" Variable Data Postcards. In this example, the best solution is to have Postcards 4"x6" as the Main Product and the other 3 should point to it as their parent.

Parent Products are used on Coupon Bundles. For example, if a coupon requires that Postcards and Business Cards get purchased at the same time to receive a discount, it shouldn't matter what flavor of postcards they are buying. Coupon Bundles are another reason why you should store extra product descriptions within a "Title Extension".

The Parent Product may also be used for artwork transfers. If someone wants to convert there business card into a postcard there is no point in showing them every derivative of postcards in the system. This way a user can change the postcard type after the artwork has been transferred with more information available to them (regarding the product derivatives). It solves an "information overload" problem.

Product Switching   
Select which products this product can be switched into from the "Edit Options" menu. For example let's say that the customer is working on 4x6 Postcards - Regular. You may want to give the customer the ability to turn their product into either 4x6 Postcards - Variable Data, or 6x9 Postcards - Regular.

It may not be a good idea to give the customer choices to switch to every possible combination of Postcards from within one menu, although you could if you wanted to. For example, after a customer switched to 4x6 Postcards - Variable they would see a new "Edit Options" menu. From there, they could switch back to 4x6 Postcards - Regular, or they could switch to 6x9 Postcards - Variable Data (which is a larger version of Variable Data postcards). Incremental changes may be less confusing, especially since adding additional options will exponentially increase the amount of choices of customer must look through.

Promotional Commands  
This is an advanced feature and the production floor may not be prepared to handle new commands entered here. When a product is scanned in production and it is about to be put into a shipping container, 0 - 4 Promotional Commands can be sent through the API. The Promotional command will not be sent through the API if the Customer is a Reseller.

If the software listening on the other end of the API is prepared to handle the command, it may launch an operation of some kind. For example it could fire a servo to a dump a brochure into the shipping container. If someone orders letterhead you could delegate a sample booklet of card-stock to be inserted for future orders.

Production operators on the floor usually do not see these commands being sent. Promotional Commands are meant to be more of a machine-to-machine language. As a poor-man's alternative to this functionality, you can add a "Production Alert" to one or more of the "Product Options".

Vendor Setup
It is possible to define up to 6 vendors for this product. Each vendor will have permission to login through the back end and access any order containing products they have been added to. Having multiple vendors allows outsourcing multiple stages of production to separate companies. Vendors will have access to automated workflow systems and can track their earnings efficiently.

Another great application of defining multiple vendors is for tracking internal costs or supplies. You can create multiple accounts in the system (using multiple email addresses) and track expenses in rough detail. For example, you may want to define vendors called "Paper Supplies", "Customer Service Labor", or maybe "Packaging". Assigning a fixed cost per product unit (such as a single business card) will allow you to see how much certain supplies are costing you at the end of the month. You can share the same Vendors between multiple products or add unique vendors for each product.

Changing Vendor ID's (on products which have already been ordered) will not change the VendorID's on the previous orders. It will only have an effect on future orders.

If you need to add/remove Vendors (or change Vendor Pricing) please ask an administrator.

Changing the Alias Name will affect all "Open Orders". If there are a lot of open orders in the system changing the Alias could take a while.


Detailed Choice Description
Enter a description that will be displayed to the user when deciding what choice to pick. The actual Choice Name should be fairly small, such as... "Glossy". You may want the user to see more details such as.... "Glossy (UV Coated)". You also can choose to make the Choice Description in HTML format, which gives you additional control to launch pop-up windows for further details, etc. The Choice Name will still show up on Customer Invoices & the backend. The Choice Description will only show up when customers are selecting their product options.

Hide Option Details?
Hide From Customer
When this choice is set to "hidden", customers will not see Details of the Option/Choice. You should generally do this when this Admin choice does not add to the Subtotal. For example if the Admin Option reads "Artwork Assistance" and this choice is called "None" then you can make the Option Label "Artwork Assistance" disappear from customer Invoices, Shopping Cart, Saved Projects, etc. Be advised that charges will still be tacked on to customer subtotals, even if the Option is hidden.

If a Option/Choice is hidden from general users, administrators will still be able to see the Production Option and modify.

Hide in Long Lists
On "Order Lists" or customer order history, you may want to hide certain option choices if they are insignificant and detract from important selections.

Quantity Price Breaks for this Option/Choice Combination
Quantity Price Breaks for this Option/Choice combination work identically to base Quantity Price Breaks, except that they are performed at another level. Quantity Price Break Changes (for Option/Choices) are calculated in addition to all other Price modifications (including price changes to the Option/Choice itself). For example, if you create a "Root Quantity Price Break" at 300 quantity (increasing the Subtotal by $5.00) and then define a Option/Choice Quantity Price Break at 300 for Card Stock -> Glossy that increases the subtotal by $3.00... then the overall subtotal will be increased by $8.00 when 300 glossy cards are ordered.
