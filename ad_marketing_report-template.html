Customers
Total Customers: {TOTAL_CUSTOMERS}
The amount of customers within the period. Reprints may count twoard a customer.

Total Customers (belonging to Sales Reps): {SALESREP_CUSTOMERS}
Total number of customers during this period belonging to sales reps (even if they have expired).

First-Time Customers: {FIRSTTIME_CUSTOMERS}
If a NEW customer places multiple orders within this period, then they will count as 1 new customer. Includes first-time customers belonging to Sales Reps too.

Repeat Customers: {REPEAT_CUSTOMERS}      - ({REPEAT_CUSTOMER_PERCENT}%)
If a NEW customer places 2 or more orders within this period then he will count as a repeat customer.
If a customer placed an order before the start time of this report and then places another order within this period, 1 repeat customer will be counted.
Re-print orders will not count towards a repeat customer.

First-Time Customers (though a Banner): {FIRSTTIME_CUSTOMERS_BANNERS}
Will be counted if a new customer has a record in the banner log and does not belong to a sales rep.
It is possible that the user may order through a banner click without a sales rep on the first order and then later become linked to them.
In this case they will not count in this statistic (unless the future order from the Sales Rep is outside of the "date range").

First-Time Customers (belonging to Sales Reps): {FIRSTTIME_SALESREP_CUSTOMERS}
It is possible that a "house customer" could later be linked to a Sales Rep.
In this case, they may show up in this statistic even though there first order wasn't necessarily the cause of a the Sales Rep. (Providing that both orders are within the "date range".)

First-Time Customers using a Coupon: {FIRSTTIME_COUPON_CUSTOMERS}
Customers that use a coupon for their first order. (coupons belonging to sales reps don't count).
It is possible for the customer to use a coupon and also have a record in the banner log. In this case they will show up in both statistics.

First-Time Nomad Customers: {NOMAD_CUSTOMERS}
Looks for NEW customers without a banner log record, they don't belong to a Sales Rep, and they haven't used a coupon.
This is could be a "word of mouth" indicator.
It may also include customers that have permanent cookies disabled (and don't complete their purchase within a single session).
It may also include customers that find the website from a second computer.

New Accounts: {NEW_ACCOUNTS}
Amount of new accounts, regardless if they place an order or not.

Same Day Account Purcahse: {SAME_DAY_ACCOUNT_PURCAHSES}      Ratio to New Accounts: {SAME_DAY_ACCOUNT_PURCAHSE_RATIO}%
In order for a same-day-account-purcahse to happen, the user must place their first order on the same day that their account was created. The ratio is built against the total New Accounts for the day.

Delayed Purcahses From Account Creation: {DELAYED_ACCOUNT_PURCAHSES}      Ratio to New Accounts: {DELAYED_ACCOUNT_PURCAHSES_RATIO}%
The farther back you run a report, the more likely this number will increase (because it gives users more time to place an order). It counts anyone that placed an order at least 1 day after the account creation.

Dead Accounts: {DEAD_ACCOUNTS}      Ratio to New Accounts: {DEAD_ACCOUNT_RATIO}%
Amount of new accounts who's owners have not placed an order (until present time).
Generating the report in the past will give more of a chance to the owners for placing orders.
